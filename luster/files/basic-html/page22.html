                   troops led by Malabar Collector Thomas, D.S.P. Hitchcock, A.S.P. Amu,

                   inspected Tirurangadi East Masjid and several Mappila houses. Pottayil
                   Mohammad Haji, Kozhissery Muhammad and Moitheenkutty were arrested
                   and questioned but no weapons were found or evidence of rioting was
                   found.This raised frustration in Thomas. The governor's representative was

                   against such rides and warned the collector that such takeaways would lead
                   to provoking the Mappilas.Although the collector and his team withdrew

                   from the raid, things began to drop as they feared. False news began to
                   spread throughout Malabar that the army had boarded with boots and
                   polluted the Tirurangadi east Masjid and demolished the Mamburam
                   Makham. The beginning of the riot was from such false news.On August 20,

                   1d 921, things were completely uncontrolled. A group led by Ali Musliyar
                   came to request the Collector demanding the release of the arrested
                   innocents. As Promising to be release, ASP Rowley asked the reporters to sit

                   down and, as per the order they sat and Rowley ordered the mob to shoot
                   them as soon as they were seated.Violent fighting followed, 17 Mappilas
                   killed in the firefight, killing seven soldiers, including Rowley and Head

                   Constable Moyteen, and the rest retreating. It was with this incident that the
                   Khilafatriots broke out inMalabar.



                   Likewise Ali Musliyar functioned a supremacy role against Mighty British
                   rule over Malabar areas. Ali Musliyas passed away on Feb 2, 1922 at

                   Coimbatore prison as a pious slave to God Allah…..
